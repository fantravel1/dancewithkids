Parent Coaching Guide

How to support, encourage, and celebrate your child's dance journey

The Parent's Mindset Shift

Before you can coach your child, shift your own thinking. Dance isn't about performing perfectly. It's about moving joyfully in your body.

What to Say (and What NOT to Say)

‚úÖ You Should Say

  • "I saw you trying that jump!"
  • "You looked like you were having fun!"
  • "You're brave for trying something new"
  • "What part did you enjoy most?"
  • "You moved your body and that's amazing"
  • "You're learning something new!"

‚ùå Don't Say

  • "You're so talented!"
  • "You're a natural!" (this implies no work needed)
  • "You're better than that other kid"
  • "Why can't you get it right?"
  • "You should be more graceful"
  • "That was perfect" (there is no perfect)

Your Child Doesn't Need a Perfect Coach

They need a cheerleader. A witness. Someone who celebrates their joy without judgment.
That's you. And you're already doing it.
