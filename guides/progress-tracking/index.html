Progress Tracking & Celebration

Notice growth, build confidence, celebrate every milestone

Why Progress Tracking Matters

Kids don't always see their own progress. They compare themselves to others instead of noticing their own growth. Your job is to notice what THEY miss and point it out.

  • Intrinsic pride (they feel proud of their own effort)

How to Track Progress

Method 1: The Simple Video Log

Record a 30-second video of them dancing the same routine monthly. Watch the progression over time.

  • Month 1: Shy, hesitant, needs guidance
  • Month 3: Confident, smiling, mostly accurate
  • Month 6: Enthusiastic, adds personal style, teaches others

Why it works: Kids can literally SEE themselves improving. Videos don't lie. This is powerful motivation.

Method 2: The Progress Journal

Keep a simple journal of observations. One entry per week. Just a few sentences.

Week 1: "Maya watched but didn't dance. That's okay. First time exposure."
Week 4: "Maya danced the last 30 seconds. She smiled!"
Week 8: "Maya did the full routine. She remembered the moves and didn't need the video."

Why it works: You'll see patterns. Growth is gradual and easy to miss week-to-week, but looking back at the journal shows the journey clearly.

Method 3: The Achievement Tracker

Create simple checkmarks or stickers for specific achievements.

  • ‚òë Completed Easy level routine
  • ‚òë Tried Moderate level
  • ‚òë Danced 3 days in a row
  • ‚òë Learned all the moves without the video
  • ‚òë Taught a friend one of our routines
  • ‚òë Stayed balanced during a 360 spin
  • ‚òë Did a full routine without pausing

Why it works: Kids see tangible progress. Checkmarks and stickers feel rewarding. Simple and visual.
