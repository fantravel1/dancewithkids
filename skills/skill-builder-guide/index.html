Skill Builder Micro-Lessons

Master one dance technique at a time. 60-120 second lessons. Build skills that last.

What Are Skill Builder Micro-Lessons?

Skill Builder micro-lessons are short, focused videos that teach one single dance technique in isolation. No choreography. No full routines. Just the pure skill.

Instead of learning 8 moves in one routine, you learn 1 move perfectly across multiple days. This is how dancers actually build mastery: repetition, isolation, and progressive challenge.

How to Use Skill Builders Effectively

The Daily Skill Building Plan

Don't try to master all 8 skills at once. Instead, focus on ONE skill per week:

Monday & Tuesday

Learn the skill basics. Watch the micro-lesson twice. Practice slowly.

Wednesday & Thursday

Practice with music. Add tempo. Feel the rhythm while doing the skill.

Friday & Weekend

Practice in a full routine. See how this skill fits into choreography.

Example: Week of Rhythm Counting

Monday: Learn 1-2-3-4 counting with the first micro-lesson. Clap the beat. Count out loud 10 times.
Tuesday: Review and practice with different tempos. Slow, then faster.
Wednesday: Add body movement. Bounce while counting.
Thursday: Counting with music. Feel the beat in a song you love.
Friday: Use rhythm counting in a full routine. Notice how the counts align with choreography.
Weekend: Practice, play, have fun. The skill is sticking.

Pro Tips

  • Repetition is your friend: Watch the same micro-lesson 5 times. Your brain needs repetition to internalize a skill.
  • Practice daily, not for hours: 5-10 minutes a day beats 60 minutes once a week.
  • Mirror work: Practice in front of a mirror. Seeing yourself helps you adjust form.
  • Film yourself: Record 30 seconds of your practice. Watch it back. Notice what you did right.
  • Celebrate progression: Week 1 you could barely balance. Week 4 you hold it for 10 seconds. That's HUGE.
