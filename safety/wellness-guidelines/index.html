Dance Wellness & Safety Guidelines

Keep your child dancing safely and joyfully. Prevention, nutrition, recovery, and when to seek help.

The Complete Warm-Up (Always Do This)

A proper warm-up prepares the body, increases heart rate safely, and prevents injury. Never skip this step.

Phase 1: General Movement (2-3 minutes)

Get the heart pumping and muscles warm with light cardio:

  • Marching in place
  • Slow jog around the room
  • Jumping jacks (modified or full)
  • Butt kicks
  • High knees

Phase 2: Dynamic Stretching (2-3 minutes)

Move through stretches actively. Don't hold; keep moving:

  • Arm circles (forward and back)
  • Leg swings (forward/back and side-to-side)
  • Gentle torso twists
  • Walking lunges
  • Hip circles
  • Shoulder rolls

Phase 3: Skill-Specific Warm-Up (1-2 minutes)

Practice the moves from today's routine at SLOW speed:

  • Do the first combination slowly, no music
  • Add music at half speed
  • Do a few practice run-throughs at normal speed

Cool-Down & Recovery (Always Do This)

A proper cool-down slows the heart rate safely, aids recovery, and prevents dizziness.

Step 1: Slow Movement (1-2 minutes)

Gradually slow down. Slow walks, gentle swaying, deep breaths. Let the heart rate come down naturally.

Step 2: Static Stretching (2-3 minutes)

Now hold stretches. The muscles are warm and flexible. Hold each 15-30 seconds:

  • Hamstring stretch (forward fold)
  • Quad stretch (pull foot toward glute)
  • Hip flexor stretch
  • Shoulder and chest stretch
  • Spinal twist

Step 3: Hydration & Rest

  • Drink water (not too much, not too fast)
  • Sit and relax for 5 minutes
  • Let heart rate return to normal before eating a heavy meal
  • Celebrate the session!

Injury Prevention & Warning Signs

Common Overuse Injuries

Most kid dance injuries come from doing too much, too fast. Prevention beats treatment:

  • Ankle sprains: Avoid uneven surfaces. Warm up ankles specifically. Land softly.
  • Knee pain: Don't push advanced moves (spins, high kicks) too early. Build strength first.
  • Lower back strain: Never force big movements. Core strength matters. Rest between intense sessions.
  • Shin splints: Common with sudden increases in intensity. Increase gradually over weeks.

When to Stop & Seek Help

These are red flags. Stop dancing and see a doctor:

  • Sharp, sudden pain (not muscle soreness)
  • Swelling or bruising that doesn't go away
  • Unable to put weight on a limb
  • Pain that gets worse with rest
  • Dizziness, nausea, or difficulty breathing
  • Any injury affecting their normal activities

Nutrition for Young Dancers

Before Dancing

  • Light snack (not a full meal): banana, crackers, yogurt
  • 1-2 hours after a big meal is ideal
  • Hydrate 30 min before
  • Avoid sugary snacks right before (energy crash after)

After Dancing

  • Protein + carbs within 30 min (helps muscle recovery): PB&J, cheese & crackers, smoothie
  • Hydrate with water, not sugary drinks
  • Full meal can wait 30 min if needed
  • Rest is part of recovery

Dance Safe, Dance Long

Following these guidelines helps your child dance safely for years. Joy comes from feeling good in your body.
