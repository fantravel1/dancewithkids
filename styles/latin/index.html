What Is Latin Dance?

A family of vibrant dance styles born from the rich cultural tapestry of Latin America and the Caribbean.

A Continent of Rhythm

Latin dance is not one style â€” it is dozens, each born from a unique mix of Indigenous, African, and European traditions. From the streets of Havana to the carnival stages of Rio, from the dance halls of Santo Domingo to the neighborhoods of Medellin, every country contributed its own rhythmic identity to this extraordinary family of dances.

Cultural Fusion

Latin dance is a living archive of cultural blending. Salsa combines Cuban son with jazz. Cumbia merges Indigenous Colombian rhythms with African percussion. Bachata fuses bolero guitar traditions with Dominican street culture. Every step your child learns carries layers of history, resilience, and creative innovation.

Latin Dance Styles We Teach

Five distinct Latin styles, each with its own character, rhythm, and magic.

Salsa

Born in the Cuban communities of New York City in the 1960s, salsa is the king of Latin dance. Its quick-quick-slow rhythm pattern, spinning turns, and infectious energy make it the perfect gateway into Latin movement. Kids learn the basic step, cross-body lead concepts, and partner awareness that form the foundation of all Latin dance.

ğŸ‡©ğŸ‡´

Merengue

The national dance of the Dominican Republic is also the easiest Latin style to learn. Its simple two-step marching pattern means kids as young as three can participate immediately. Despite its simplicity, merengue builds crucial skills â€” hip movement, arm styling, and the confidence to move with a partner or in a group.

ğŸ‡©ğŸ‡´

Bachata

Also from the Dominican Republic, bachata features a distinctive side-to-side step pattern with a tap on every fourth beat. The guitar-driven music is warm and melodic, and the dance emphasizes smooth hip motion and emotional expression. Kids learn to move with feeling, connecting body movement to musical storytelling.

ğŸ‡¨ğŸ‡´

Cumbia

Colombia's gift to the world, cumbia features a shuffling sideways step pattern that mimics the movement of waves on the Caribbean coast. The music blends Indigenous flutes, African drums, and Spanish melodies into something entirely unique. Kids love cumbia's playful, circular movement patterns and its welcoming, celebratory spirit.

Reggaeton

The newest member of the Latin dance family, reggaeton emerged from Puerto Rico in the 1990s. Its dembow rhythm drives modern dance culture worldwide. We adapt reggaeton choreography to be 100% kid-appropriate while keeping the energy, confidence, and urban swagger that makes it so popular with young dancers everywhere.

Skills You'll Learn in Latin Dance

Footwork, rhythm, and the art of dancing with others â€” Latin dance builds skills for life.

Precise Footwork

Latin dance lives in the feet. Kids master step patterns that range from simple marches to intricate syncopated sequences, building coordination and spatial awareness with every routine.

Partner Awareness

Many Latin dances are social â€” designed for two people. Kids learn to communicate through movement, respect personal space, lead and follow, and create something beautiful together with another dancer.

Hip Motion & Body Roll

The signature Cuban motion â€” a figure-eight hip pattern driven by bending and straightening the knees â€” is the foundation of Latin dance. Kids develop core stability, flexibility, and fluid movement naturally.

Clave Rhythm Mastery

The clave is the heartbeat of Latin music â€” a five-note rhythmic pattern that organizes everything else. Kids learn to hear the clave in any song, unlocking the ability to dance authentically to any Latin music.

ğŸ”„

Turns & Spins

From simple quarter turns to full spins and even multiple rotations, Latin dance teaches balance, spotting technique, and the exhilarating feeling of controlled momentum through space.

Arm Styling & Flair

Graceful arm movements add personality and polish to Latin dance. Kids learn to extend, wave, and frame their movements with arms that complement rather than compete with their footwork.

Fun Facts About Latin Dance

Fascinating stories from the world of Latin dance that kids love to share.

Salsa Has No Single Birthplace

Salsa was not invented in one place â€” it evolved simultaneously in Cuba, Puerto Rico, New York, and Colombia, with each region adding its own flavor. That is why there are so many salsa styles: LA style, New York style, Cuban casino, and Colombian cali-style, each with unique patterns and energy.

ğŸ‰

The World's Biggest Dance Party

The Cali Fair in Colombia holds the record for some of the largest salsa dance gatherings on Earth, with over 100,000 dancers filling the streets. Latin dance festivals happen in every major city worldwide, proving that rhythm knows no borders.

ğŸ“º

Reggaeton Changed Everything

Reggaeton's dembow rhythm, born from Jamaican dancehall via Panama and Puerto Rico, has become the most-streamed music genre globally. Artists like Daddy Yankee, Bad Bunny, and Shakira brought Latin dance to billions of viewers, making Latin movement a universal language.

ğŸ¥

African Roots Run Deep

Almost every Latin dance traces significant roots to African rhythms and movement patterns brought to the Americas through the transatlantic diaspora. The polyrhythmic complexity, hip-centered movement, and communal spirit of Latin dance are direct inheritances from West and Central African traditions.
