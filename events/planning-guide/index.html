Dance Event Planning Guide

Everything you need to host a successful, joyful, safe dance event for your school, family, or community.

Pre-Event Planning (4-6 Weeks Before)

Define Your Event

  • Event Type: School assembly? Family event? Community celebration? Classroom showcase?
  • Audience: How many people? What age groups?
  • Duration: 15 minutes? 30? Full hour?
  • Goal: Entertainment? Learning? Bonding? Fundraiser?
  • Budget: Do you need AV equipment rental? Sound system? Decorations?

Select Routines

  • Choose 2-3 routines that fit your audience and event length
  • Mix difficulty levels if multiple age groups will perform
  • Consider rehearsal time needed (Easy/Moderate routines = fewer rehearsals)
  • Avoid 6+ minute routines for kids' attention spans
  • Pre-watch every routine. Know the music, the moves, and what to expect

Rehearsal Phase (2-4 Weeks Before)

Sample 4-Week Rehearsal Schedule

Week 1: Learn Basics

Meet the group. Watch full routine. Learn individual sections. Focus on comfort and familiarity.

Week 2: Build Confidence

Practice full routine 2-3x per week. Add some music. Celebrate progress.

Week 3: Polish & Perform

Full run-throughs with costume/setup. In-venue rehearsal if possible.

Week 4: Prepare for Success

Rest before the big day. One final run-through. Build excitement.

Rehearsal Best Practices

  • Start each rehearsal with confidence: Review what they already know before teaching new material.
  • Use counts, not just beats: "1-2-3-4" helps everyone stay together.
  • Make it fun: Games, friendly competitions, celebration. Not military-style drills.
  • Meet them where they are: Some kids pick it up fast; others need more practice. Both are normal.
  • Never force participation: If a kid is nervous, they can observe or do a simplified version. Forcing creates trauma.
  • Hydration breaks: Movement makes kids thirsty. Have water available.

Event Day Setup

2 Hours Before

  • Arrive early. Test ALL audio/visual equipment. Not working? You have time to fix it.
  • Sound check: Play your routine audio at full volume. Adjust levels.
  • Check lighting: Can audience see the stage clearly? Any glare on screens?
  • Mark performer positions on stage with tape if needed
  • Have backup audio on phone AND computer (redundancy saves the day)

30 Minutes Before

  • Gather performers. Go to the designated area away from audience view
  • Do ONE full run-through. Correct any major issues only
  • Remind them: "You've practiced this. You know these moves. Have fun and smile."
  • No last-minute choreography changes. Kids need confidence, not confusion
  • Bathroom breaks completed NOW, not during performance

During Performance

  • Play the video on cue. Make sure audio/video sync correctly
  • Stand to the side where performers can see you if they need reassurance
  • Smile. Clap. Be the energy you want in the room
  • If something goes wrong (music cuts out, person freezes), keep going. Most audience won't notice
  • Record it (audio or video). These memories matter

After the Event

Celebrate & Reflect

  • Celebrate EVERYONE: Performers, crew, audience, everyone. This was a community effort.
  • Ask performers how they felt: Not "did you do good?" but "what did you feel when you saw everyone smiling?"
  • Share video with families: These moments are precious. Let parents relive them.
  • Thank your team: Teachers, volunteers, anyone who helped. Gratitude matters.
  • Document feedback: Ask families and performers: What worked? What would you improve? Use this for next time.
