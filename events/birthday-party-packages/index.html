Step-by-Step Birthday Party Guide

Before the Party (1 week)

  1. Pick your package: Choose by birthday kid's age and interests
  2. Watch the routines yourself: Know what you're playing. It helps you run it smoothly
  3. Tell the birthday kid: Build excitement. "We're having a dance party!"
  4. Prepare the space: Clear furniture, ensure good lighting, test audio
  5. Tell guests: Optional but helpful: "We'll be dancing! Wear comfy clothes"

During the Party (Actual Timeline)

5-10 min: Greetings & Settling In

Kids arrive. Light snack if you want. Let them settle and chat.

10-15 min: Dance Party Begins

"We're going to dance together! No sitting out, everyone moves." Press play. Lead first routine. Have FUN.

15-25 min: Full Dance Set

Play 3-5 routines. Mix difficulty. Let birthday kid pick one. Keep energy high.

25-30 min: Cool Down & Celebrate

Do a slower, celebratory final routine. Clap. Cheer. "You all danced! You're amazing!"

30+: Cake, Games, Rest

Kids are tired and happy. Move to cake and other activities.

Pro Tips for Success

  • You lead the moves, especially for younger kids. If adults dance, kids feel safe dancing.
  • No pressure. If a kid wants to sit out or freestyle, that's fine.
  • Keep it short. 20-30 minutes of dancing is perfect. Longer = exhaustion and meltdowns.
  • Music volume matters. Loud enough to hear, not so loud it's overwhelming.
  • Celebrate everyone. "Look at all these great dancers!" Not "You're the best."
  • Have a backup plan. If energy is too high, do slower routines. If it's too low, switch to faster ones.
