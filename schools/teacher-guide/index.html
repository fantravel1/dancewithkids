Sample 4-Week PE Unit: "Hip Hop Fundamentals"

Here's how a teacher might structure a 4-week unit using DanceWithKids:

Week 1: Foundation & Footwork

Routines: "Basic Bounce" (Easy), "Hip Hop Basics Pt. 1" (Moderate)
Focus: Rhythm counting, basic hip hop footwork, beat awareness
Activity: Learn moves, practice 3x, write reflection on rhythm

Week 2: Arm Isolation & Body Control

Routines: "Arm Waves" (Skill Builder), "Hip Hop Basics Pt. 2" (Moderate)
Focus: Arm isolation, body awareness, coordination
Activity: Practice isolations, combine with footwork, peer feedback

Week 3: Combo Building

Routines: "Groove Street Foundation" (Hard), "Hip Hop Basics Pt. 3" (Moderate)
Focus: Combining moves, transitions, performance readiness
Activity: Build personal combo, practice with music, record for portfolio

Week 4: Performance & Reflection

Routines: Any completed routine of choice (all levels)
Focus: Performance confidence, self-assessment, goal-setting
Activity: Class showcase, written reflection on progress, next-level goal

Classroom Management Tips

üéØ Start Small

Use Easy level routines first, even with older students. Build confidence before adding complexity. A confident 6th grader doing easy moves beats a frustrated 6th grader attempting hard moves.

‚úÖ Set Clear Expectations

Establish routines: when you press play, everyone participates. No phones, no sitting out. Create a safe, non-judgmental space where effort is celebrated.

üì∫ Position Your Screen Well

Projector or large TV where everyone can see the dancer clearly. Bright classroom? Mirror-mode might be hard. Use full screen, large text, and clear audio.

üéµ Use It for Transitions

Use quick 1-minute routines between lessons. It energizes students, breaks monotony, and gives their brains a change-of-pace. Perfect for focus and ADHD support.

üí¨ Celebrate All Effort

Shy student moved at all? Celebrate. Incorrect moves? Doesn't matter ‚Äî the goal is joy and confidence. Create a culture where trying is the win.

üì± Adapt for Your Space

Small classroom? Use "seated" or "quiet mode" routines. Limited space? Choose routines marked as "small-space friendly." DanceWithKids adapts to your reality.
